At a young age, I found that I was always wondering about how things worked. The challenge of assembling furniture without even the smallest glance at the instructions was something that excited me. It's no wonder that my initial major in college was mechanical engineering. Although my journey took me down a path in business marketing/pr and aviation, my curiosity never weaned.

My curiosity has taken me on a path of continual learning to explore new skills. With that and my passion to create and contribute to the creation of things, the world of development seemed like an obvious next step. I was weary at first, with tech being such a male dominanted field, but after spending time learning Ruby, my first coding language, I felt like I had found my calling.

I hope to get to a point where I can become a mentor to young women of color and show that they too can enter and flourish in the world of tech.
